Dates of Operation

We're open from November 29 - December 31, pending no storms. The display will end in advance of any major storm. We have the following hours:

  • Sunday - Thursday: 4:30p to 9:30p ET
  • Friday and Saturday: 4:30p to 10:00p ET

The busiest days of the week are Friday, Saturday, and Sunday. Long lines, especially during these days, may result in a truncated show. We encourage you to come during a weeknight for the best experience. Below is a calendar indicating our forecast for the busiest and least-busiest nights. Use it to help plan your visit!

INCLEMENT WEATHER POLICY: During periods of snow, rain, or other inclement weather, the show may be shut down. Please keep an eye on our home page and Facebook page for the latest updates. Generally, snow is not an issue for the show: rain creates many more problems for the lights.

Pay It Forward

Each year, Luke's Lights organizes a project for viewers to give back to our local community. In previous years, we have sponsored a food drive; this year, our campaign is Pay It Forward, a low-to-no-cost activity with many ways to participate.

Monetary donation: donate to a 501(c)(3) non-profit organization.
Food drive donation: donate to your local food pantry or food drive. Please note: Luke's Lights will not be collecting food on property in 2020.
Act of kindness: spread positivity in the community by performing acts of kindness (e.g. holding the door open for someone, paying for the next person's coffee in the drive-thru, or volunteering time with a non-profit).

Rules & Regulations

As always, the safety of viewers and our neighbors is our highest priority. This year, as a result of the global health crisis, we ask that at all times, viewers keep their windows up, doors closed, and watch the show from inside their cars by tuning their radios to 106.9 FM.

Additionally, we have the following rules, and we greatly appreciate your assistance in following them. Thank you!

  • Do not block or turn around in our neighbors' driveways. Our considerate neighbors allow us to continue this show each year, and we want to keep them happy!
  • Keep one lane open for other cars to pass. We must prioritize neighborhood traffic.
  • Move as far forward as possible when there is traffic behind you. This maximizes how many people can view our show at once, which keeps traffic flowing and prevents us from needing to shorten the show.
  • Do not beep your horn.
  • Do not litter. Littering can cause an electrical problem.
  • Consider turning off your lights. Not all cars can; however, if yours can, please turn them off for a more immersive experience.
  • Consider turning off your engine if you plan on staying for an extended period of time. Bring blankets; it gets cold!
  • Consider participating in Pay It Forward to help the local community. See more details above.
  • And lastly, enjoy the show! When seeing the show, feel free to tweet us or post on our Facebook page! After you've seen the show, please take the poll on our home page to tell us which song you liked the most!
